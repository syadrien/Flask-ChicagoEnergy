Prediction of energy use by buildings in Chicago



TDI Capstone project of Adrien SY







The buildings sector accounts for about 76% of electricity use and 40% of all U. S. primary energy use and associated greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions. It is therefore essential to reduce energy consumption in buildings in order to meet national energy and environmental challenges and to reduce costs to building owners and tenants. As one of the largest cities in the United States, Chicago have a lot of opportunity to unlock energy and cost savings opportunities for businesses and residents by improving energy efficiency. In this capstone project, I aim to investigate and predict the energy use of building in Chicago using the Chicago Energy Benchmarking dataset. This constitute valuable information for City-Level Energy Decision Making.


The dataset : 2014-2018 Chicago Energy Benchmarking data
